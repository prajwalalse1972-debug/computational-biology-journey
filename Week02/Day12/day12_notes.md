Membrane proteins must interact with the non-polar interior of the phospholipid bilayer.

- The fatty acid tails of membrane lipids are hydrophobic.
- Transmembrane regions therefore contain long stretches of non-polar amino acids.
- Hydrophobic residues allow thermodynamic stability inside the lipid environment.
- Without sufficient hydrophobic residues, the protein would be energetically unfavorable within the membrane.

Hydrophobicity is one of the primary driving forces of protein folding.

- **Hydrophobic collapse**: Non-polar side chains cluster together to avoid water.
- **Core formation**: Proteins often form a dense hydrophobic core in the center.
- Hydrophilic residues remain exposed on the surface to interact with water.
- This arrangement stabilizes the protein‚Äôs 3D structure.

Soluble proteins must remain dissolved in aqueous environments such as cytoplasm or blood.

- Hydrophilic surfaces allow interaction with water.
- Charged and polar residues prevent aggregation.
- While the surface is typically hydrophilic, active sites may contain hydrophobic pockets for specific substrate binding.
